# 1.7 Online and Automated Sample Preparation for Direct Infusion

Everything so far has assumed that your cleaned-up sample eventually flows through a separation. But a large and growing fraction of intact-protein and native MS is done by **direct infusion** — spraying a purified sample straight into the source with no online chromatography. When you do that, the entire burden shifts onto sample preparation: the protein has to already be concentrated, desalted, and sitting in an MS-compatible (volatile) buffer by the time it reaches the tip. Doing that by hand, one sample at a time, is slow and irreproducible. The last decade has produced automated, online front-ends that do it in seconds and then walk away, and they are worth knowing about even if your day-to-day work is LC-based.

## SampleStream

**SampleStream** is a microfluidic front-end that couples directly to an electrospray source, developed in Neil Kelleher's group at Northwestern University and commercialized by Integrated Protein Technologies. Instead of separating analytes chromatographically, it performs rapid in-line processing — concentration of dilute samples and online buffer exchange/desalting — and then delivers the cleaned, concentrated protein to the ESI source as a discrete plug. The founding paper reports throughput as fast as ~15 seconds per sample, compatibility with both native and denaturing electrospray, and sensitivity comparable to LC-MS.[^park2020] The point is to remove the chromatographic step — and its speed, carryover, and salt-tolerance bottlenecks — while still cleaning up MS-incompatible (high-salt, dilute, complex-matrix) samples, so that intact-protein experiments can run automated and unattended at population scale.

For top-down and proteoform work specifically, the key demonstration couples immunoprecipitation to the platform (IP-SampleStream-MS): applied to serum from 25 individuals, it quantified apolipoprotein A-I and C-III proteoforms with more than seven-fold higher throughput and better analytical variation than LC-MS, and reproduced known proteoform-to-phenotype associations.[^seckler2021] The platform was later integrated with Orbitrap-based charge-detection (individual-ion) MS and scaled up to megadalton assemblies such as adeno-associated virus (AAV) capsids.[^janisse2025] Its heaviest uptake, though, has been in biopharma: end-to-end automated intact-mass workflows have screened and characterized hundreds of bispecific and multispecific antibodies in roughly ten working days.[^niu2024]

## Chip-based and robotic nanoESI infusion

The other route to automated direct infusion is robotic, chip-based nanoelectrospray. The enabling hardware is a microfabricated silicon chip etched with nanoelectrospray nozzles, which produces stable, reproducible nanospray by continuous infusion at nanoliter-per-minute flow rates.[^schultz2000] This chip became the heart of the commercial Advion TriVersa NanoMate. Automating it for proteins removes one of direct infusion's worst headaches — carryover: the robot aspirates each sample from a 96-well plate with a disposable tip and sprays it from a fresh, unused nozzle, so there is simply no shared fluid path to contaminate the next sample.[^zhang2003] For native intact-protein MS, the automated chip was used to record spectra of the noncovalent transthyretin tetramer with roughly ten-fold greater signal stability than a pulled nanoflow capillary, to observe thyroxine binding, and to screen small-molecule stabilizers — establishing chip infusion as a practical route to higher-throughput native MS and ligand screening.[^keetch2003] The advantages are a consistent set: replaceable nozzles (no carryover), walk-away operation, long-lasting stable spray from microliter volumes, and sample economy. In practice the NanoMate is also frequently used to infuse fractions collected from an upstream separation, decoupling acquisition time from the chromatographic timescale.

## Online buffer exchange and desalting for native MS

For native MS in particular, the dominant automated front-end is **online buffer exchange (OBE)**: a sample sitting in a non-volatile buffer is injected onto a short size-exclusion column run with an aqueous, non-denaturing *volatile* mobile phase, so the protein or complex elutes separated from its salts and sprays directly into the instrument. The landmark protocol formalized rapid OBE for screening proteins, protein complexes, and even clarified cell lysates, replacing slow, hard-to-automate offline desalting (spin columns, dialysis, offline SEC) and enabling sub-five-minute quality control.[^vanaernum2020] Conceptually related work had earlier coupled robust automated online SEC to high-resolution native MS for quantitative protein–ligand affinity screening, benchmarked against chip-based nanoESI infusion as the reference method.[^ren2019] OBE was subsequently adapted to the hardest case — membrane proteins — with mobile phases compatible with detergent micelles and with nanodiscs, completing each measurement in minutes; this connects directly to the challenges in [§1.5 — Handling Membrane Proteins and Large Targets](./membrane-large-proteins.md).[^liu2023]

The honest summary: direct infusion trades chromatographic separation for speed, automation, and simplicity, so it shines when your sample is already simple — a purified protein, an immunoprecipitation eluate, a single complex — and it struggles with complex mixtures, where you still want the LC or CZE approaches from the earlier sections. Match the front-end to the sample: SampleStream and chip infusion for throughput and automation, online buffer exchange for native complexes trapped in difficult buffers.
